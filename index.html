Our Guide

CPR

For people not breathing or with no pulse, Involves chest compressions and rescue breaths.

Choking

Uses back blows and the Heimlich maneuver to dislodge objects from the airway.

Burns

Cooling the burn with water, covering it with a clean cloth, and seeking medical help if severe.

Care

Cool the burn with water (not ice), cover with a sterile cloth, and avoid popping blisters. For severe burns, call for medical help.

Safty Precautions

Personal Safety First

Assess the environment: Before approaching the injured person, check for any hazards like traffic, fire, electrical wires, or other dangers that might put you at risk.
Wear protective gloves: If available, use disposable gloves to protect yourself from bloodborne pathogens or infections.

Protect the Injured Person

Ensure the person is in a safe location: Move them away from any dangers, if possible, without causing further injury.
Do not move them excessively: Only move them if their life is in immediate danger (e.g., from a fire or oncoming traffic).

Monitor the Person:

vitals: Monitor breathing, pulse, and consciousness level while waiting for help. Comfort and reassure: Stay with the person and offer emotional support until help arrives.

Use the Right First Aid Equipment:

well-stocked first aid kit: Familiarize yourself with the items in the kit and their proper usage. Use items correctly: For example, don’t use a tourniquet unless absolutely necessary.
